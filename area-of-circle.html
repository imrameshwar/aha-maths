How does it work?

The formula Area = πr² isn't magic — it's geometry you can see. The interactive above lets you feel the proof, step by step.

1
Start with a circle. Radius r, area unknown. We need to figure out what πr² even means.
2
Slice it like pizza. Cut the circle into N equal wedges — thin triangular sectors. The more slices, the thinner each wedge.
3
Rearrange the wedges. Flip alternating wedges and lay them in a row — tips up, tips down, interlocking. You get a bumpy parallelogram.
4
Take the limit. As N → ∞, the bumpy edges flatten. The parallelogram becomes a perfect rectangle.
5
Read the rectangle. Its height is r (the radius). Its width is half the circumference — πr. So Area = πr × r = πr². Bas itna simple tha!

Drag the Slices slider in the interactive above and watch the bumpy top and bottom edges flatten into a straight line as N increases. That's the limit argument — not a formula to memorise, but a shape you can feel.
